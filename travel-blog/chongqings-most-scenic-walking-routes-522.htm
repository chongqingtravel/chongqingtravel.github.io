Chongqing’s Most Scenic Walking Routes

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Chongqing, China’s sprawling megacity, is a place where modernity collides with natural beauty. Known for its dramatic hills, winding rivers, and misty skies, this city offers some of the most breathtaking urban hikes in the world. Whether you're a traveler seeking hidden gems or a local looking to rediscover your city, these scenic walking routes will take you through Chongqing’s most iconic landscapes, historic neighborhoods, and vibrant street life.

The Best Urban Walks in Chongqing

1. Hongyadong to Chaotianmen: A Riverside Stroll Through History

One of Chongqing’s most famous landmarks, Hongyadong, is a great starting point for a scenic walk. This stilted riverside complex, inspired by traditional Bayu architecture, is a maze of shops, teahouses, and restaurants. From here, follow the Jialing River toward Chaotianmen, where the Jialing meets the Yangtze.

  • Highlights:
    • The contrast of old wooden structures against the futuristic skyline.
    • Street vendors selling spicy Chongqing snacks like xiaomian (spicy noodles).
    • The Chaotianmen Wharf, a historic port with panoramic river views.

This route is especially magical at night when Hongyadong’s lights reflect on the water.

2. Liziba Light Rail Walk: A Surreal Urban Hike

Chongqing’s monorail system is an attraction in itself, but the stretch near Liziba Station offers a unique walking experience. The rail cuts right through a residential building, creating an iconic photo op. Start at Fotuguan Station and walk downhill toward Liziba, passing through narrow alleys and staircases that define Chongqing’s hilly terrain.

  • Highlights:
    • Watching the monorail pass through the apartment building (best viewed from the Liziba Observation Deck).
    • Exploring hidden hutong-style streets with local eateries.
    • The Eling Park detour for a panoramic city view.

3. Nanshan Mountain Trail: A Nature Escape with Skyline Views

For those craving greenery, Nanshan Mountain is a must. The trails here wind through dense forests, leading to viewpoints like One Tree Hill and Nanshan Botanical Garden. The hike is moderately challenging but rewards you with sweeping vistas of Chongqing’s skyline.

  • Highlights:
    • The Nanshan Observatory, perfect for sunset photography.
    • Hidden temples like Laojun Cave Temple tucked into the hills.
    • The option to extend the hike to Tushan, another scenic peak.

Hidden Gems: Off-the-Beaten-Path Walks

4. Ciqikou Backstreets: Beyond the Tourist Crowds

Ciqikou Ancient Town is a well-known tourist spot, but the real charm lies in its quieter alleys. Instead of sticking to the main street, venture into the residential lanes where locals play mahjong and artisans craft pottery.

  • Highlights:
    • Baolun Temple, a serene Buddhist temple away from the bustle.
    • Traditional teahouses where you can sip puerh tea.
    • The lesser-known riverside path leading to small fishing villages.

5. Huangjueping Art District: Street Art & Staircase Walks

Chongqing’s answer to Berlin’s graffiti scene, Huangjueping is a vibrant art district built along a steep hillside. The Sichuan Fine Arts Institute is the heart of this area, with murals covering entire buildings. The walk here involves climbing staircases adorned with colorful art.

  • Highlights:
    • The Tank Loft Creative Park, a hub for indie galleries.
    • The 108-step staircase, a photogenic urban climb.
    • Cafés and bookshops frequented by local artists.

Chongqing’s Most Challenging Hike: The City Wall Trail

For history buffs and adventure seekers, the Chongqing Ancient City Wall Trail is a fascinating but strenuous walk. This route follows remnants of the Ming Dynasty city walls, some of which are hidden in modern neighborhoods.

  • Route Tips:
    • Start at Tongyuan Gate and follow markers to Dongshui Gate.
    • Expect steep climbs and uneven steps—wear good shoes!
    • Pass by Donghua Temple, an underrated historical site.

Night Walks: Chongqing After Dark

6. Nanbin Road: The Best Nighttime Skyline Walk

Chongqing’s skyline is even more impressive when lit up at night. Nanbin Road, along the Yangtze River, offers unobstructed views of the city’s neon skyline, including the Raffles City complex.

  • Highlights:
    • The light show on the riverside buildings.
    • Open-air bars with huoguo (hot pot) and craft beer.
    • A river cruise option for a different perspective.

7. Jiefangbei Pedestrian Street: A Neon Wonderland

No visit to Chongqing is complete without experiencing Jiefangbei, the city’s bustling commercial heart. By night, the giant LED screens and glowing skyscrapers create a cyberpunk atmosphere.

  • Highlights:
    • The People’s Liberation Monument, a historic landmark.
    • Late-night shopping and street food stalls.
    • Nearby Bayi Road Snack Street for midnight bites.

Practical Tips for Walking in Chongqing

  • Wear comfortable shoes—expect lots of stairs and hills.
  • Use a map app like Amap or Google Maps (with VPN), as streets can be confusing.
  • Stay hydrated—Chongqing’s spicy cuisine can be intense!
  • Avoid midday heat in summer—early mornings or evenings are best.

Chongqing is a city best explored on foot, where every turn reveals a new surprise—whether it’s a hidden temple, a dizzying staircase, or a breathtaking skyline view. Lace up your shoes and start walking!
