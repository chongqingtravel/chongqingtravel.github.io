Chongqing’s Top Picnic Spots in Nature

Chongqing, a sprawling megacity in southwestern China, is often celebrated for its futuristic skyline, spicy hotpot, and labyrinthine urban layout. Yet, beyond the neon-lit streets and the iconic Hongya Cave lies a side of Chongqing that many travelers overlook—its breathtaking natural landscapes. For those seeking a respite from the city’s relentless energy, the surrounding mountains, lakes, and forests offer some of the most stunning picnic spots in the country. Whether you are a local looking for a weekend getaway or a tourist eager to explore beyond the guidebooks, this guide will walk you through the top picnic destinations in nature around Chongqing, complete with tips on what to bring, how to get there, and what makes each location unique.

Nanshan Mountain: The Urban Oasis with Panoramic Views

Why Nanshan Mountain Stands Out

Nanshan Mountain, or Southern Mountain, is one of the most accessible natural escapes from downtown Chongqing. Located just a 30-minute drive from the city center, this lush green area offers a perfect blend of convenience and tranquility. The mountain is famous for its tea plantations, ancient temples, and, most notably, its panoramic views of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers converging below. For a picnic, the area around the Nanshan Botanical Garden is ideal. The garden itself is a sprawling 500-acre park filled with exotic plants, shaded pathways, and quiet nooks perfect for laying out a blanket.

Best Picnic Spots on Nanshan

The Tea Plantation Terrace
One of the most picturesque spots is the tea plantation terrace near the Laojun Cave. The terraced fields create a natural amphitheater, and the rows of green tea bushes provide a serene backdrop. Early mornings are particularly magical here, as the mist rolls in from the river below, giving the entire scene a dreamlike quality. Bring a thermos of hot tea and some light snacks, and you can enjoy a peaceful morning surrounded by the scent of fresh leaves.

The Viewing Platform at Yikeshu
For those who want a bit more action, the viewing platform at Yikeshu (literally “One Tree”) offers a stunning vantage point. From here, you can see the entire Chongqing skyline, including the iconic Raffles City complex and the twisting bridges that crisscross the rivers. The platform is often less crowded during weekdays, making it a great spot for a late afternoon picnic as the sun begins to set. The golden hour here is nothing short of spectacular, with the city lights gradually flickering on as darkness falls.

Practical Tips for Nanshan

Getting to Nanshan is straightforward. You can take a taxi from downtown for around 50 RMB, or use the city’s extensive bus network. Bus routes 346 and 384 both stop near the main entrance. If you are driving, parking is available but can get crowded on weekends. Remember to bring insect repellent, as the mountain is home to a variety of bugs, especially during the humid summer months. Also, pack a light jacket—the temperature on the mountain can be several degrees cooler than in the city.

Fairy Mountain National Forest Park: A Fairytale Escape in Wulong

The Magic of Wulong

If you are willing to venture a bit further from the city, Fairy Mountain National Forest Park in Wulong County is an absolute must-visit. Located about three hours by bus from Chongqing, this UNESCO World Heritage site is famous for its dramatic karst landscapes, lush meadows, and the iconic Three Natural Bridges. The park is vast, covering over 130 square kilometers, so you will have no trouble finding a secluded spot for a picnic. The area is also known for its unique microclimate, which often creates a misty, ethereal atmosphere that feels straight out of a fantasy novel.

Top Picnic Locations in Fairy Mountain

The Grassland of Xiannvshan
The central grassland, known as Xiannvshan, is the most popular picnic area. The rolling green hills are dotted with wildflowers in spring and summer, and the air is crisp and clean. You can rent a small tent or simply spread a blanket on the grass. The best part? The grassland is surrounded by towering limestone peaks that seem to rise out of nowhere, creating a dramatic backdrop for your meal. Pack a hearty lunch, such as sandwiches and fruit, and enjoy the sounds of birds and the distant rustle of wind through the trees.

The Lakeside Area Near the Three Natural Bridges
For a more unique experience, head to the lakeside area near the Three Natural Bridges. These massive stone arches, formed by centuries of erosion, are one of the park’s main attractions. The lake itself is calm and reflective, perfect for a quiet picnic. You can also take a short boat ride across the water to reach a small island with picnic tables. This spot is less crowded than the grassland, making it ideal for those seeking solitude. Just be aware that the path to the lake involves some stairs, so pack light.

How to Get to Fairy Mountain

The easiest way to reach Wulong is by taking a high-speed train from Chongqing North Station to Wulong Station. The journey takes about 50 minutes and costs around 50 RMB. From the station, you can take a local bus or taxi to the park entrance. The park itself charges an entrance fee of around 120 RMB, but the experience is well worth the cost. If you plan to stay overnight, there are several guesthouses and hotels near the park, though booking in advance is recommended during peak season.

Jinfo Mountain: The Sacred Peak with Ancient Forests

A Spiritual Connection with Nature

Jinfo Mountain, located in Nanchuan District, is one of the most sacred mountains in the region, often referred to as the “Buddhist Mountain” due to its numerous temples and monasteries. The mountain rises to an elevation of over 2,200 meters, making it significantly cooler than the city below. The forests here are ancient, with some trees estimated to be over a thousand years old. For a picnic, the area around the Jinfo Temple is particularly serene, offering a mix of cultural history and natural beauty.

Prime Picnic Areas

The Bamboo Grove Near the Temple
Just a short walk from the Jinfo Temple, there is a dense bamboo grove that feels like a natural cathedral. The tall bamboo stalks create a canopy that filters the sunlight, casting dappled shadows on the ground. The sound of the wind moving through the leaves is incredibly calming. This is a great spot for a simple picnic of rice balls and pickled vegetables, traditional Chongqing snacks that pair well with the tranquil setting.

The Summit Viewpoint
For those who are up for a hike, the summit of Jinfo Mountain offers a 360-degree view of the surrounding landscape. The trail to the top is well-maintained but steep, taking about an hour to complete. Once you reach the top, you will find a flat area with a few benches and stone tables. The view is breathtaking, with layer upon layer of mountains fading into the horizon. Pack a lunch that is easy to carry, such as wraps or dumplings, and enjoy your meal while watching the clouds drift below you.

Travel Tips for Jinfo Mountain

Jinfo Mountain is about a two-hour drive from Chongqing. You can take a bus from the Chongqing Long-distance Bus Station to Nanchuan, then transfer to a local bus to the mountain. The entrance fee is around 90 RMB, and there is a cable car that can take you part of the way up for an additional fee. The weather on the mountain can change quickly, so bring layers and a rain jacket. Also, note that the mountain is a popular destination for pilgrims, so weekends can be busy. Visiting on a weekday will give you a more peaceful experience.

Chongqing’s Countryside: Hidden Gems in the Suburbs

The Beauty of the Rural Landscape

While the major attractions are well-known, some of the best picnic spots in Chongqing are hidden in the countryside just outside the city. These areas offer a glimpse into rural life in Sichuan, with terraced rice fields, small villages, and winding rivers. One such area is the region around the town of Beibei, about an hour north of the city center. Beibei is known for its hot springs and the Jinyun Mountain, but the surrounding farmland is equally charming.

A Picnic by the Jialing River

The Riverside Meadows
Just outside Beibei, there are several spots along the Jialing River where the banks are wide and grassy. These meadows are perfect for a picnic, especially in the spring when the wildflowers are in bloom. You can watch fishermen casting their lines, or simply relax and listen to the water flow. The area is not heavily touristed, so you will likely have the place to yourself. Pack a picnic basket with local specialties like spicy beef jerky and cold noodles, and enjoy a quiet afternoon by the river.

The Village of Pianyan
A bit further afield, the village of Pianyan in the Tongnan District is a hidden gem. This ancient village is built along a canal and is known for its traditional architecture and peaceful atmosphere. The surrounding fields are perfect for a picnic, and you can also explore the narrow alleyways and old bridges. The village is easily accessible by bus from Chongqing, and there are several small restaurants where you can buy freshly made snacks to take with you.

Getting Off the Beaten Path

To explore these rural areas, consider renting a car or hiring a driver for the day. This will give you the flexibility to stop wherever you find a beautiful spot. Alternatively, you can take a local bus to Beibei or Tongnan and then walk or rent a bicycle. These areas are not as developed as the major attractions, so be prepared for basic facilities. However, the authenticity and tranquility of the countryside make it well worth the effort.

What to Pack for a Perfect Picnic in Chongqing’s Nature

Essentials for Comfort

When planning a picnic in Chongqing’s natural areas, preparation is key. The weather can be unpredictable, especially in the mountains, so always bring a light jacket or sweater. A waterproof blanket is also a good idea, as the ground can be damp, especially in the mornings. Sunscreen and a hat are essential for sunny days, while insect repellent is a must for any outdoor activity in this region.

Food and Drink Ideas

Chongqing is famous for its spicy cuisine, and a picnic is a great opportunity to enjoy local flavors. Consider packing items like liangfen (cold jelly noodles), mapo tofu (in a sealed container), or chuanchuan (skewers) that are easy to eat on the go. For drinks, a thermos of hot tea is perfect for cooler days, while cold beer or a bottle of local Baijiu can add a festive touch. Don’t forget to bring plenty of water, as hiking can be dehydrating.

Leave No Trace

One of the most important aspects of enjoying nature is respecting it. Always pack out what you pack in, and dispose of trash properly. Many of these natural areas are protected, and littering can harm the environment. Bring reusable containers and utensils to minimize waste. By being a responsible visitor, you help ensure that these beautiful spots remain pristine for future generations.

Seasonal Considerations: When to Go

Spring and Autumn: The Ideal Seasons

The best times for a picnic in Chongqing’s nature are spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). During these months, the weather is mild, with temperatures ranging from 15 to 25 degrees Celsius. The landscapes are at their most vibrant, with cherry blossoms in spring and golden leaves in autumn. The humidity is also lower, making outdoor activities more comfortable.

Summer: Embrace the Heat

Summer in Chongqing can be brutally hot, with temperatures often exceeding 35 degrees Celsius. However, if you choose a mountain destination like Jinfo or Fairy Mountain, the higher elevation provides a welcome escape from the heat. The summer months also bring lush greenery and blooming wildflowers. Just be sure to start your picnic early in the morning to avoid the midday sun, and bring plenty of water.

Winter: A Quiet Retreat

Winter is the least popular time for picnics, but it can be magical if you are prepared for the cold. The mountains are often dusted with snow, creating a serene, almost otherworldly atmosphere. Nanshan and Jinfo Mountain are particularly beautiful in winter, with fewer crowds and a peaceful silence. Pack warm clothing and hot soup in a thermos to stay comfortable.

The Cultural Aspect: Picnicking Like a Local

The Tradition of Yechu

In Chongqing, the concept of a picnic is often tied to yechu, which literally means “going out into the wild.” This is a beloved local tradition where families and friends gather in natural settings to cook and eat together. Unlike a Western picnic, which might focus on cold sandwiches and salads, a Chongqing-style yechu often involves portable stoves, hotpot ingredients, and an array of grilled meats. While this requires more preparation, it is a fantastic way to experience the local culture. Many of the spots mentioned in this guide have designated areas for cooking, so you can bring your own portable stove and enjoy a hotpot under the open sky.

Sharing Food and Stories

Picnicking in Chongqing is as much about the social experience as it is about the food. Locals often bring multiple dishes to share, and the meal can last for hours. Don’t be surprised if you are invited to join a nearby group—Chongqing people are known for their hospitality. If you are traveling solo, consider bringing extra snacks to share, as this can be a great way to connect with locals and learn more about the area.

Final Thoughts on Chongqing’s Natural Picnic Spots

Chongqing’s natural landscapes offer a diverse range of picnic experiences, from the easily accessible Nanshan Mountain to the remote beauty of Fairy Mountain. Each location has its own unique charm, whether it is the panoramic city views, the ancient forests, or the peaceful countryside. By planning ahead and respecting the environment, you can create unforgettable memories in these stunning settings. So pack your basket, grab your blanket, and head out to explore the wild side of Chongqing. The city’s natural wonders are waiting to be discovered.
