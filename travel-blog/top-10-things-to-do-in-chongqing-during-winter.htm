Top 10 Things to Do in Chongqing During Winter

Chongqing in winter is a city transformed. The legendary summer furnace cools to a crisp, atmospheric dreamscape. The perpetual mist, now laced with a chilly breath, clings to the steep hillsides, turning the sprawling metropolis into a scene from a cyberpunk fantasy. Forget the idea of hibernating; winter unlocks a unique side of this megacity. The fiery hotpot tastes richer, the neon lights glow brighter against the early dusk, and the cultural experiences feel more intimate. Here are the top 10 ways to experience the unparalleled magic of Chongqing during the cooler months.

1. Dive into a Spicy Hotpot Haven

Winter in Chongqing is synonymous with hotpot. The colder it gets, the more essential this communal, boiling cauldron of flavor becomes.

Find Your Perfect Pot

Seek out the local favorites tucked away in alleyways. The classic Hongyou (red oil) broth, numbingly spicy with Sichuan peppercorns and chili, is a rite of passage. For a less intense start, try the Yuanyang (Mandarin Duck) pot, split between spicy and mild broths like a savory tomato or bone broth. The key is in the dipping sauce: sesame oil, garlic, and cilantro to cool the bite.

Winter-Specific Delicacies

This is the season for premium ingredients. Thinly sliced mutton and beef, fresh Napa cabbage, and earthy mushrooms are classics. Don’t miss the local specialties: crunchy duck intestine, tender beef aorta, and glutinous rice cakes that soak up the sublime broth. The steam rising from the pot, fogging up the windows of a bustling restaurant, is the quintessential Chongqing winter experience.

2. Witness the Surreal Hongyadong at Night

The stilted pavilions of Hongyadong, built into the cliffside over the Jialing River, are spectacular year-round, but winter adds a layer of drama. As night falls early, the entire complex ignites in a golden glow, its reflection shimmering on the dark river below. The mist often floats through, making the towers appear like a ghostly, ancient palace suspended in the air. Wander through its 11 floors of shops and eateries, feeling the contrast between the chilly river air and the warm, lively interior. It’s a living postcard of old and new Chongqing.

3. Cruise the Two Rivers Through the Mist

A Yangtze River cruise is a summer staple, but a winter cruise offers a profoundly different, more ethereal adventure.

A Moody, Cinematic Journey

Bundle up and stand on the deck as the boat glides through the famous confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers. The winter fog creates a dynamic, ever-changing scene. The city’s skyscrapers, like the iconic Liberation Monument complex, play peek-a-boo through the haze. The bridges—each an architectural marvel—emerge dramatically as you pass beneath them. This perspective of Chongqing as a "city in the clouds" is at its most potent in winter.

4. Soak in a Hot Spring with a View

Escape the urban chill by embracing one of Chongqing’s greatest geographical gifts: natural hot springs. The Northern Hot Springs in Beibei or the newer resorts around the city offer a blissful retreat.

Alfresco Relaxation

Imagine soaking in a steaming, mineral-rich pool while cool air brushes your face. Many springs are set in scenic hillsides, offering stunning views of mist-shrouded forests or river valleys. It’s the perfect antidote to a day of walking the hilly streets and a luxurious way to warm up from the inside out.

5. Explore Ciqikou Ancient Town Without the Crowds

This preserved old town is often packed, but winter offers a chance to experience its ancient charm more peacefully. The narrow, stone-paved lanes feel more atmospheric under grey skies. Savor a cup of locally grown tea in a quiet teahouse, watch artisans make Sujuan (Shu embroidery), and snack on freshly made Chen Mahua (a twisted dough snack). The smell of roasting nuts and sesame oil fills the crisp air, making for a wonderfully sensory stroll back in time.

6. Ride the Monorail Through Apartment Buildings

Chongqing’s mountainous terrain has led to some of the world’s most unique transit solutions. The most famous is Line 2, where the train plunges directly through the middle of a residential high-rise at Liziba Station. In winter, the view from inside the warm train car is especially fascinating as you zip through the urban canyon, with glimpses of daily life in the apartments and the misty city unfolding below. It’s a must-do for any urban explorer or photography enthusiast.

7. Visit the Dazu Rock Carvings in Solemn Silence

A short trip from the city center, the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Dazu Rock Carvings is even more compelling in the off-season. The intricate Buddhist, Confucian, and Taoist sculptures carved into the hillsides feel more solemn and mystical without summer crowds. The cool, quiet atmosphere allows for undisturbed contemplation of these千年-old masterpieces. The subtle colors and expressions of the statues seem to tell their stories more vividly in the winter light.

8. Get Lost (and Found) in a "Labyrinth" City

Chongqing’s 3D topography is an attraction in itself. Winter is the perfect time to embrace urban exploration.

Discover Hidden Stairways and Elevated Walkways

Stroll along the **Shancheng* (Mountain City) Step Path, a historic trail that weaves through old neighborhoods, offering surprising vistas. Visit the **Ronghui* Elevated Walkway, a network of pedestrian sky-bridges that let you navigate entire city blocks without ever touching the ground. The lack of summer heat makes these walks genuinely enjoyable adventures.

9. Indulge in Street Food on *Nanshan*

Nanshan (South Mountain) is a popular scenic area, but in winter, it becomes a cozy food destination. The many restaurants and street stalls here serve up hearty, warming fare. Enjoy a sizzling plate of La Zi Ji (diced chicken with dried chilies), a bowl of rich Xiao Mian (Chongqing small noodles), or sweet, sticky Tang You Baba (glutinous rice balls). The view of the city lights from Nanshan on a clear winter night, while warming your hands with a delicious snack, is unforgettable.

10. Experience the *Jiefangbei* CBD Holiday Lights

From late December through Chinese New Year, the heart of Chongqing—the Liberation Monument Central Business District—transforms into a dazzling winter wonderland. The towering skyscrapers are adorned with spectacular light shows and festive decorations. The square itself often features themed installations and markets. The energy is electric, with a festive buzz in the cool air. It’s a fantastic opportunity to see Chongqing’s ultra-modern, cosmopolitan side in full celebratory swing, a perfect blend of traditional holiday spirit and futuristic spectacle.
