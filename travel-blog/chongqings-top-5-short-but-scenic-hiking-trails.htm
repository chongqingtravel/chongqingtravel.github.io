Chongqing’s Top 5 Short but Scenic Hiking Trails

Nestled among the steep hills and lush river valleys of southwestern China, Chongqing is a city of dramatic contrasts—futuristic skylines rising from misty mountains, spicy hotpot aromas blending with fresh forest air, and ancient cultural sites hidden just beyond bustling urban centers. While many travelers come for the food or the iconic cityscapes, an growing number of outdoor enthusiasts are discovering Chongqing’s incredible network of hiking trails. These paths offer not only physical challenge but also a profound sense of connection to nature and history.

What makes these trails particularly appealing is their accessibility. You don’t need to be an expert hiker or have days to spare. Many of Chongqing’s best routes are short—typically doable in half a day—but incredibly rich in scenery, culture, and moments of awe. From panoramic city views to serene forest walks and glimpses into ancient Buddhist traditions, these trails capture the essence of Chongqing without demanding a major time commitment.

For those looking to escape the urban energy for a few hours, here are five of the best short but spectacular hiking trails in and around Chongqing.

Nanshan Mountain Trail: Skyline Views and Fresh Air

Just across the Yangtze River from Chongqing’s bustling core, Nanshan Mountain offers a quick nature retreat with some of the best views of the city. The entire area is a lush forest park, but the well-marked trails along the ridge provide the perfect mix of natural beauty and urban vistas.

Trail Overview and Highlights

The most popular route is a 5-kilometer loop that takes about 2 to 3 hours at a leisurely pace. The path is well-maintained, with stone steps and dirt tracks winding through dense groves of pine and bamboo. As you climb, you’ll reach several lookout points that offer breathtaking views of Chongqing’s iconic skyline, including the magnificent convergence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers.

One of the highlights is the One Tree Pavilion, a classic Chinese viewing platform that seems to float above the forest. It’s the perfect spot for photography, especially in the early morning when the city is often shrouded in ethereal fog, or at night when the skyscrapers light up like a scene from a cyberpunk novel.

Cultural Touchpoints and Tips

Nanshan is also home to several temples and historical sites. The Nanshan Taoist Temple is a serene stop along the way, where the scent of incense blends with the mountain air. For a post-hike treat, head to one of the local restaurants near the mountain base for authentic Chongqing chicken or a refreshing bowl of ice jelly (bingfen).

The best time to hike is on a weekday to avoid crowds. Bring water and wear good shoes—the steps can be steep, but the reward is more than worth it.

Ciqikou to Gele Mountain Trail: History Meets Nature

This trail combines two of Chongqing’s most beloved cultural landmarks with a refreshing climb into the green hills. Starting from the ancient streets of Ciqikou Old Town, you’ll journey upward into Gele Mountain, a site of profound historical and spiritual significance.

Starting in Ciqikou

Begin your day exploring the narrow, cobbled streets of Ciqikou, a preserved ancient town that was once a busy commercial port on the Jialing River. Grab a souvenir or sample some local snacks like maoxuewang (blood curd stew) or twisted dough sticks. From there, follow the signs leading toward Gele Mountain. The transition from the noisy, vibrant streets into the quiet forest feels like stepping into another world.

The Climb and the Sights

The hiking path is approximately 4 kilometers one way, with a moderate incline. The trail is shaded by trees and offers occasional glimpses back toward the river and the old town below. The destination is the summit of Gele Mountain, home to the historic Luohan Temple and the larger Baoding Mountain Buddha carvings. The temple, dating back to the Song Dynasty, is an active monastery where you can observe Buddhist ceremonies and admire ancient stone sculptures.

The hike takes about 2 hours to ascend, and you can either return the same way or take a public bus back to the city. This trail is ideal for those who want a dose of culture with their nature walk.

Jinyun Mountain National Nature Reserve: A Biodiversity Hotspot

For a deeper immersion into nature without leaving the city, Jinyun Mountain is unparalleled. This protected area is one of Chongqing’s “green lungs,” famous for its rich biodiversity, rare plants, and tranquil atmosphere. The trails here are peaceful, well-marked, and perfect for a half-day escape.

Why Jinyun is Special

Jinyun Mountain boasts over 2,000 species of plants, including ancient ferns and rare orchids. It’s also a haven for birdwatchers and butterfly enthusiasts. The main trail system consists of several interconnected loops, so you can customize your hike from 3 to 8 kilometers depending on your energy level.

Must-See Spots on the Trail

Don’t miss the Lion Peak, the highest point on the mountain, which offers a stunning 360-degree view of the Beibei district and the surrounding Jialing River Valley. Another highlight is the Xianglu Peak, a quieter spot ideal for meditation or a picnic. Along the way, you’ll pass by clear streams, small waterfalls, and lush bamboo forests that make you forget you’re just a short drive from a megacity.

The reserve has an entrance fee, and it’s open from early morning to late afternoon. Go on a sunny day to see the sunlight filtering through the dense canopy—it’s a magical experience.

Tieshanping Forest Park: Family-Friendly Adventure

If you’re hiking with family or prefer a gentler, more relaxed trail, Tieshanping Forest Park is the perfect choice. Located in the Yubei District, this vast park features flat, paved paths that wind through beautiful forests, around lakes, and past artistic installations.

Ease and Accessibility

The main loop is about 5 kilometers long and virtually flat, making it suitable for children, seniors, or anyone looking for a stroll rather than a strenuous climb. The park is designed with leisure in mind, with plenty of benches, picnic areas, and even coffee shops along the way.

Artistic and Natural Blend

What sets Tieshanping apart is its integration of art into the natural environment. You’ll encounter large-scale sculptures, modern art installations, and poetic inscriptions carved into stones throughout the forest. It feels like walking through an open-air museum. The Moon Lake within the park is particularly picturesque, with clear waters reflecting the surrounding trees and sky.

Visit in autumn when the leaves change color, creating a vibrant tapestry of reds and yellows. It’s a popular spot for local photographers and Instagrammers.

Simian Mountain Trail (Partial Route): A Taste of Wilderness

For those willing to venture a bit farther from the city center, the Simian Mountain range offers some of the most dramatic landscapes in Chongqing Municipality. While the full mountain trek can take days, there are shorter sections that are accessible and incredibly rewarding.

Why Simian Mountain?

Simian Mountain is known for its red sandstone cliffs, deep valleys, and waterfalls. It’s often called the “Yellow Mountain of Chongqing” for its breathtaking scenery. A popular short route is the Waterfall Loop, a 6-kilometer trail that takes you to several stunning waterfalls, including the Wangxiangtai Waterfall, one of the tallest in the region.

Hiking the Waterfall Loop

The trail involves some uphill climbing and descending, but the paths are well-constructed with guardrails and viewing platforms. The sound of rushing water accompanies much of the hike, and the mist from the waterfalls cools the air—a welcome relief on a warm day. You’ll also cross charming wooden bridges and pass through dense forest filled with the songs of native birds.

This trail is about a 2-hour drive from central Chongqing, so it’s best to start early or consider staying overnight in the nearby town of Jiangjin. The effort to get there is absolutely justified by the natural beauty.

Whether you’re a casual walker or an avid hiker, these trails showcase the diverse and stunning landscapes that make Chongqing one of China’s most unique destinations. Each path tells a story—of history, nature, culture, or modern art—and invites you to become part of it, even if just for a few hours.
