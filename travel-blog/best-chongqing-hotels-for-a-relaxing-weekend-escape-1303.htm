Best Chongqing Hotels for a Relaxing Weekend Escape

Chongqing, a sprawling metropolis nestled in southwestern China, is a city of contrasts—where towering skyscrapers meet misty mountains and the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers weave through urban landscapes. Known for its fiery hotpot, vibrant nightlife, and rich cultural heritage, it’s also an ideal destination for a weekend retreat. Whether you're seeking luxury, boutique charm, or serene riverside views, Chongqing offers a variety of hotels perfect for unwinding. Here’s a curated list of the best hotels for a relaxing weekend escape.

Luxury Stays: Indulge in Opulence

The Ritz-Carlton, Chongqing

Perched atop the 58th floor of the Chongqing Tower, The Ritz-Carlton offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city and rivers. The hotel’s elegant interiors blend modern sophistication with subtle Chinese influences. Guests can unwind at the spa, take a dip in the infinity pool, or enjoy Cantonese delicacies at Li Xuan. The club lounge provides an exclusive retreat with personalized service—ideal for those who crave privacy and pampering.

InterContinental Chongqing Raffles City

This architectural marvel is part of the Raffles City complex, featuring the iconic "Horizontal Skyscraper." The InterContinental boasts spacious rooms with floor-to-ceiling windows, a state-of-the-art fitness center, and an indoor pool. The on-site restaurants serve everything from Sichuan cuisine to international fare. For ultimate relaxation, book a treatment at the AHI Spa, where traditional Chinese therapies meet contemporary wellness techniques.

Boutique Gems: Unique and Intimate

NICCOLO Chongqing

Located in the heart of the bustling Guanyinqiao district, NICCOLO combines urban chic with understated luxury. The minimalist design, curated art pieces, and plush bedding create a calming atmosphere. The hotel’s signature restaurant, Niccolo Kitchen, offers a farm-to-table dining experience, while the rooftop bar, The Lounge, is perfect for sunset cocktails with a skyline backdrop.

Somerset JieFangBei Chongqing

For travelers who prefer apartment-style accommodations, Somerset provides a home-away-from-home experience. The serviced residences feature fully equipped kitchens, cozy living spaces, and attentive concierge services. Its central location near Jiefangbei Square means you’re steps away from shopping, dining, and entertainment—yet the hotel itself remains a peaceful haven.

Riverside Retreats: Serenity by the Water

Hilton Chongqing

Overlooking the Yangtze River, the Hilton offers a tranquil escape with easy access to the city’s attractions. The rooms are designed for comfort, with soft lighting and ergonomic workspaces. Guests can enjoy a leisurely afternoon tea at the lobby lounge or savor fresh seafood at FLARE. The riverside promenade is perfect for a morning jog or an evening stroll.

Sheraton Chongqing Hotel

Situated in Nan’an District, the Sheraton boasts stunning river views and lush gardens. The hotel’s signature Sweet Sleeper® beds ensure a restful night, while the Shine Spa offers rejuvenating treatments. Don’t miss the chance to dine at Feast, where an extensive buffet showcases global flavors.

Hidden Oases: Off-the-Beaten-Path

Banyan Tree Chongqing Beibei

Tucked away in the Beibei District, this resort is a sanctuary of tranquility. Surrounded by lush greenery and natural hot springs, it’s perfect for those seeking solitude. Each villa features a private pool and outdoor terrace. The Banyan Tree Spa is renowned for its holistic therapies—try the signature Rainforest Experience for ultimate relaxation.

The Westin Chongqing Liberation Square

Though centrally located, The Westin feels like a retreat with its Heavenly® Spa and indoor heated pool. The rooms are designed to promote wellness, with air purification systems and blackout curtains. The hotel’s Cantonese restaurant, Seasonal Tastes, is a must-visit for dim sum lovers.

Budget-Friendly Comfort: Affordable Yet Cozy

Holiday Inn Express Chongqing Jiefangbei

For travelers watching their wallets without sacrificing comfort, this Holiday Inn Express delivers. The modern rooms are compact but well-appointed, and the complimentary breakfast buffet is a great way to start the day. Its proximity to public transport makes exploring the city a breeze.

Atour Hotel Chongqing Guanyinqiao

Atour blends affordability with boutique flair. The rooms feature smart lighting, high-quality linens, and a selection of books for leisurely reading. The 24-hour snack bar and cozy lobby lounge make it a great choice for late-night arrivals.

Final Tips for Your Chongqing Weekend

  • Book a Hotpot Experience: No trip to Chongqing is complete without indulging in its famed spicy hotpot. Many hotels can arrange reservations at top local spots.
  • Explore Hongya Cave: This stilted riverside complex is a must-see, especially at night when it’s illuminated.
  • Take a River Cruise: A sunset cruise along the Yangtze offers stunning views of the city’s skyline.

Whether you’re soaking in a hot spring at Banyan Tree or sipping cocktails at NICCOLO’s rooftop bar, Chongqing’s hotels provide the perfect backdrop for a rejuvenating weekend escape.
