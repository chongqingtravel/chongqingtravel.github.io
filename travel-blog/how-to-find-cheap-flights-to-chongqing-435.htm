How to Find Cheap Flights to Chongqing

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Chongqing, China’s sprawling megacity nestled along the Yangtze River, is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a mix of urban energy, spicy hotpot, and breathtaking natural scenery. Whether you're drawn to the futuristic skyline of Hongya Cave, the dramatic Three Gorges, or the mouth-numbing flavors of Chongqing cuisine, getting there doesn’t have to break the bank. Here’s your ultimate guide to scoring the cheapest flights to Chongqing while staying ahead of travel trends.

Timing Is Everything: When to Book and Fly

Book Early (But Not Too Early)

Airlines often release flight tickets about 11 months in advance, but the sweet spot for booking cheap flights is typically 3–4 months before departure. For peak seasons like Chinese New Year (late January to February) or October’s Golden Week, aim to book 5–6 months ahead to avoid skyrocketing prices.

Fly During Off-Peak Seasons

Chongqing’s high season runs from April to October, when the weather is mild and ideal for exploring. However, flying during November to March (excluding major holidays) can save you 20–40% on airfare. Bonus: You’ll avoid crowds at top attractions like Ciqikou Ancient Town.

Midweek Magic

Tuesdays and Wednesdays are statistically the cheapest days to fly. Airlines often lower prices midweek when demand dips. If your schedule allows, avoid weekends and Fridays, when prices spike.

Hacking Flight Search Tools

Use Aggregators and Alerts

Platforms like Google Flights, Skyscanner, and Kayak let you compare prices across airlines and set up price alerts. Pro tip: Use Skyscanner’s "Whole Month" view to pinpoint the cheapest travel dates.

Try Nearby Airports

Chongqing Jiangbei International Airport (CKG) is the main hub, but check flights to Chengdu (CTU) or Guiyang (KWE), which are a short high-speed train ride away. Sometimes, a multi-city combo (e.g., fly into Chengdu, train to Chongqing) saves hundreds.

Embrace Layovers

Nonstop flights are convenient, but opting for a layover (especially in hubs like Beijing, Shanghai, or Guangzhou) can slash prices. Just ensure your connection is long enough to account for delays.

Loyalty Programs and Hidden Discounts

Leverage Frequent Flyer Miles

Sign up for airline loyalty programs like China Southern’s Sky Pearl Club or Air China’s PhoenixMiles. Even if you don’t fly often, accumulating miles through credit card partnerships or dining programs can lead to free upgrades or discounted tickets.

Student and Youth Discounts

Websites like StudentUniverse or STA Travel offer exclusive deals for students and travelers under 26. Some airlines, like Air China, also provide youth fares—just verify age restrictions.

Credit Card Perks

Travel credit cards (e.g., Chase Sapphire Preferred, Capital One Venture) often include airline fee credits, lounge access, or bonus miles for signing up. Use these to offset costs or book flights through their portals for extra savings.

Local Travel Hacks for Chongqing

Pair Flights with Attractions

Some travel agencies bundle flights with hotel stays or tours to Chongqing’s top spots (e.g., Dazu Rock Carvings or Wulong Karst). These packages can be cheaper than booking separately.

Follow Chinese Booking Platforms

For last-minute deals, check Ctrip (Trip.com), Qunar, or Fliggy—they often list unadvertised discounts on domestic and international routes to Chongqing.

Watch for Airline Sales

Chinese carriers like Sichuan Airlines and China Eastern frequently run flash sales, especially around holidays like Singles’ Day (November 11) or Labor Day. Follow them on social media for real-time updates.

Avoiding Common Pitfalls

Skip the "Direct Booking" Myth

While booking directly with airlines can simplify changes, third-party sites sometimes offer lower prices. Just read cancellation policies carefully—some discount tickets are nonrefundable.

Beware of Dynamic Pricing

Airlines adjust fares based on demand and browsing history. Use incognito mode when searching and clear cookies to avoid price hikes.

Double-Check Visa Requirements

If you’re transiting through China, verify whether you need a visa. A 24- or 144-hour transit visa might apply, but rules vary by nationality and airport.

Final Pro Tips

  • Subscribe to newsletters: Airline and deal-finding newsletters (e.g., Scott’s Cheap Flights, The Flight Deal) often spotlight Chongqing routes.
  • Consider budget airlines: While Chongqing isn’t a hub for low-cost carriers, airlines like Spring Airlines occasionally offer deals from Southeast Asian cities.
  • Be flexible: If you can adjust your travel dates by a day or two, you might unlock significantly lower fares.

With these strategies, you’ll land in Chongqing ready to dive into its neon-lit streets, misty mountains, and fiery cuisine—all without draining your travel fund. Happy flying!
