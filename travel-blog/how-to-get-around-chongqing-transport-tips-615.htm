How to Get Around Chongqing: Transport Tips

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Chongqing, China’s sprawling megacity, is a mesmerizing blend of modern skyscrapers, ancient alleyways, and dramatic mountain landscapes. Navigating this hilly metropolis can be both thrilling and challenging due to its unique topography. Whether you're exploring the neon-lit streets of Jiefangbei or venturing into the lush countryside, understanding Chongqing’s transport system is key to a smooth trip. Here’s your ultimate guide to getting around Chongqing like a pro.

Public Transportation: The Backbone of Chongqing

Metro (Light Rail)

Chongqing’s metro system is one of the most efficient ways to travel, especially for tourists. The city’s light rail lines (Lines 1, 2, 3, 6, and the Loop Line) cover major attractions, including:

  • Line 2: Connects downtown areas like Jiefangbei to cultural hotspots such as Liziba Station, where the train famously passes through a residential building.
  • Line 3: Runs from Jiangbei Airport to the city center and beyond, making it ideal for airport transfers.
  • Line 6: Offers stunning views of the Yangtze River and stops at Hongyadong, a must-visit stilted complex.

Pro Tip: Avoid rush hours (7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM) if you want a more comfortable ride.

Buses

Chongqing’s bus network is extensive but can be confusing for non-Mandarin speakers. Key routes to know:

  • Route 181: Links Chaotianmen (the Yangtze River cruise hub) to major shopping districts.
  • Route 261: Connects Ciqikou Ancient Town to Shapingba’s commercial area.

Note: Buses are cashless—download the "Chongqing Public Transport" app or use a transportation card.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing: Fast but Tricky

Taxis

Taxis are affordable (starting at ¥10) but be prepared for:

  • Language barriers: Have your destination written in Chinese or pinned on a map.
  • Traffic jams: Downtown areas like Yuzhong District get congested during peak hours.

Ride-Hailing Apps

Didi Chuxing (China’s Uber) is widely used. For English-friendly options, try:

  • Meituan Dache: Offers ride-hailing with an English interface.
  • Caocao Car: An eco-friendly electric car service.

Warning: Avoid unlicensed cabs—always check the driver’s ID on the app.

Unique Modes of Transport

Cable Cars

Chongqing’s iconic Yangtze River Cableway (长江索道) is both a tourist attraction and a practical crossing. It offers panoramic views of the city’s skyline and connects Nan’an District to Yuzhong.

Hours: 7:30 AM–10:30 PM
Cost: ¥20 (single trip), ¥30 (round trip)

Monorail

The Stilted Monorail (Line 2) is a photogenic way to see Chongqing’s mountainous terrain. Don’t miss the "train-in-a-building" moment at Liziba Station.

Ferries & Cruises

For a scenic route, hop on a Yangtze River ferry from Chaotianmen Pier. Options include:

  • Public ferries (¥5–¥10): Short hops to Nanbin Road or Dadukou.
  • Night cruises (¥150+): Lavish dinner boats with skyline views.

Biking & Walking

Biking

Chongqing’s hills make cycling tough, but flat areas like Nanbin Road offer bike rentals. Use Mobike or HelloBike via their apps.

Walking

Exploring on foot is rewarding but exhausting due to steep streets. Wear comfortable shoes and check out:

  • Hongyadong: A maze of staircases and shops built into a cliff.
  • Shancheng Alley: Historic pathways with hidden teahouses.

Airport Transfers

Jiangbei International Airport (CKG)

  • Metro: Line 3 takes 30–40 minutes to downtown (¥6).
  • Taxi: A 20-minute ride costs ¥50–¥70.

Essential Apps for Navigation

  • Amap (Gaode): Real-time transit updates in Chinese.
  • Google Maps: Limited functionality; download offline maps.
  • WeChat/Weixin: Use "Mini Programs" for metro maps.

Final Tips

  • Transportation Card: Get a "Chongqing Tong" card for metro/bus discounts.
  • Avoid Rush Hour: Traffic jams are brutal—plan accordingly.
  • Ask Locals: Chongqingers are friendly and often help lost tourists.

With these tips, you’ll master Chongqing’s transport maze and focus on enjoying its hotpot, history, and breathtaking vistas. Happy travels!
