Chongqing on a Budget: Essential Items to Save Money

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Chongqing, China’s sprawling megacity, is a dream destination for travelers who love vibrant street life, spicy hotpot, and breathtaking landscapes. But like any major city, costs can add up quickly if you’re not careful. The good news? With some smart planning and a few budget-friendly essentials, you can experience the best of Chongqing without breaking the bank. Here’s your ultimate guide to saving money while exploring this dynamic city.

Transportation: Getting Around for Less

Use the Metro System

Chongqing’s metro is not only efficient but also incredibly affordable. With fares starting at just 2 RMB (about $0.30), it’s the cheapest way to navigate the city’s hilly terrain. Lines 1, 2, 3, and 6 cover most tourist hotspots, including Jiefangbei, Hongya Cave, and Ciqikou. Pro tip: Avoid taxis during rush hour—traffic jams can make fares skyrocket.

Opt for Shared Bikes

If you’re comfortable with two wheels, apps like Meituan Bike or Hello Bike offer cheap rentals. A 30-minute ride costs as little as 1.5 RMB, making it a great way to explore areas like Nanbin Road or the Yangtze River waterfront.

Walk When Possible

Chongqing’s unique topography means you’ll often stumble upon hidden gems just by wandering. Areas like Liziba (where the metro passes through a residential building) or the old streets of Shancheng Alley are best explored on foot.

Food: Eat Like a Local Without Overspending

Street Food is Your Best Friend

Skip the fancy restaurants and head straight to street vendors. Some must-try budget bites include:
- Xiaomian (Chongqing Noodles): A bowl of spicy, savory noodles costs around 6-10 RMB.
- Suan La Fen (Hot and Sour Rice Noodles): A local favorite for under 8 RMB.
- Jianbing (Savoury Pancakes): Perfect for breakfast at just 5 RMB.

Visit Local Markets

Head to Caixi Market or Xinghua Market for fresh produce, snacks, and even pre-cooked meals at a fraction of restaurant prices. Grab a Liang Fen (Cold Jelly Noodle) for 5 RMB or a Baozi (Steamed Buns) for 2 RMB each.

Share Hotpot with Friends

Chongqing hotpot is a must, but dining alone can be pricey. Team up with fellow travelers or locals to split the cost. Budget-friendly chains like Xiaolongkan or Liuyishou offer set menus for under 50 RMB per person.

Accommodation: Sleep Cheap but Comfortable

Hostels and Guesthouses

Areas like Jiefangbei or Hongya Cave have plenty of budget hostels with dorm beds starting at 40 RMB/night. Look for highly-rated spots like Chongqing Panda International Hostel or Wanderlust Hostel for a social atmosphere.

Capsule Hotels

For ultra-budget travelers, capsule hotels like Sleep No More offer private pods for around 60 RMB/night—great if you just need a clean place to crash.

Couchsurfing

If you’re open to meeting locals, platforms like Couchsurfing can score you free stays while giving you insider tips on the city.

Sightseeing: Free and Low-Cost Attractions

Explore Free Landmarks

  • Hongya Cave: This iconic stilted building is free to wander (though some shops inside are pricey).
  • Ci Qi Kou (Porcelain Village): A charming ancient town with free entry (just avoid overpriced souvenir shops).
  • Chongqing People’s Square: A great spot to people-watch and see local dance performances.

Affordable Paid Attractions

  • Yangtze River Cable Car: At 20 RMB for a one-way ticket, it’s a steal for panoramic views.
  • Eling Park: Entry is just 5 RMB, and the view of the city skyline is unbeatable.

Take Advantage of Free Walking Tours

Some hostels and local groups offer free walking tours—just tip your guide if you enjoy the experience.

Shopping: Bargain Like a Pro

Skip the Tourist Traps

Avoid shopping at hotspots like Jiefangbei’s luxury malls. Instead, head to Chaotianmen Market for wholesale prices on clothing, souvenirs, and electronics.

Haggle at Night Markets

The Nanbin Road Night Market and Shapingba Market are perfect for bargaining. Start by offering half the asking price and negotiate from there.

Miscellaneous Money-Saving Tips

Use Alipay or WeChat Pay

Many vendors offer small discounts for digital payments. Plus, you’ll avoid ATM fees by loading money onto these apps in advance.

Carry a Reusable Water Bottle

Tap water isn’t drinkable, but most hostels and metro stations have filtered water dispensers. Save the 3-5 RMB per bottle!

Travel Off-Peak

Visiting in shoulder seasons (March-April or September-October) means cheaper flights and accommodations.

Learn Basic Mandarin Phrases

While many young locals speak English, knowing phrases like “Duo shao qian?” (How much?) or “Tai gui le!” (Too expensive!) can help you score better deals.

Chongqing doesn’t have to be expensive. With these tips, you’ll enjoy the city’s fiery cuisine, stunning views, and rich culture—all while keeping your wallet happy. Happy travels!
