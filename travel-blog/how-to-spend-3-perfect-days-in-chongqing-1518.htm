How to Spend 3 Perfect Days in Chongqing

Chongqing, China’s sprawling megacity, is a mesmerizing blend of modernity and tradition, perched on the steep hillsides where the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers converge. Known for its spicy hotpot, futuristic skyline, and rich cultural heritage, this city offers an unforgettable experience. Here’s how to make the most of three perfect days in Chongqing.

Day 1: Exploring the Heart of the City

Morning: Start with a Stroll at Ciqikou Ancient Town

Kick off your Chongqing adventure at Ciqikou, a well-preserved ancient town with Ming and Qing dynasty architecture. Wander through narrow alleys lined with teahouses, souvenir shops, and street food stalls. Don’t miss trying Chongqing xiaomian (spicy noodles) or jiangtuan fish for an authentic local breakfast.

Afternoon: Discover Hongya Cave and Jiefangbei

Head to Hongya Cave, an iconic stilted building complex that lights up spectacularly at night. During the day, explore its maze-like shops selling handicrafts and snacks. Then, make your way to Jiefangbei, Chongqing’s bustling commercial center. This pedestrian square is surrounded by luxury malls and historic landmarks—perfect for shopping and people-watching.

Evening: Cruise the Yangtze River

Cap off your first day with a night cruise on the Yangtze River. The city’s skyline, illuminated by neon lights, reflects magically on the water. Opt for a dinner cruise to enjoy local delicacies while gliding past landmarks like the Chaotianmen Gate and Qiansimen Bridge.

Day 2: Nature and Culture Immersion

Morning: Visit the Three Gorges Museum

Begin your second day at the Three Gorges Museum, where you’ll learn about the region’s history, the Yangtze River, and the massive Three Gorges Dam project. The museum’s exhibits include ancient artifacts, folk art, and a fascinating scale model of the dam.

Afternoon: Escape to Eling Park and Liziba

Next, head to Eling Park, the highest point in central Chongqing, offering panoramic views of the city. Afterward, visit Liziba Station to see Chongqing’s famous "train-through-building" phenomenon—a monorail that passes directly through a residential complex. It’s a surreal sight and a must for photography enthusiasts.

Evening: Feast on Chongqing Hotpot

No trip to Chongqing is complete without indulging in its legendary hotpot. Head to a local favorite like Xiaolongkan or Da Long Yi for a fiery broth loaded with Sichuan peppercorns and chili. Pair it with huanghou (beef tripe) and mutton slices for the full experience.

Day 3: Adventure Beyond the City

Morning: Day Trip to Dazu Rock Carvings

Take a day trip to the Dazu Rock Carvings, a UNESCO World Heritage Site about two hours from Chongqing. These intricate Buddhist sculptures, dating back to the 9th century, are carved into cliffs and caves, showcasing incredible artistry and spiritual devotion.

Afternoon: Explore Wulong Karst National Park

If time allows, venture to Wulong Karst National Park, famous for its dramatic limestone formations and the Three Natural Bridges. This otherworldly landscape was even featured in Transformers: Age of Extinction. Walk through the towering arches and lush valleys for a breathtaking nature experience.

Evening: Relax at Nanshan Botanical Garden

Return to the city and unwind at Nanshan Botanical Garden, especially beautiful at sunset. The garden features themed sections, including a cherry blossom area and a bamboo forest, making it a serene escape from the urban hustle.

Chongqing is a city of contrasts—where ancient traditions meet cutting-edge innovation, and fiery flavors balance serene landscapes. Whether you’re a foodie, history buff, or nature lover, these three days will leave you enchanted by this dynamic metropolis.
