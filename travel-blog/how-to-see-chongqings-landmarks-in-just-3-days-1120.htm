How to See Chongqing’s Landmarks in Just 3 Days

Chongqing, China’s sprawling megacity, is a mesmerizing blend of modernity and tradition, nestled among misty mountains and split by the mighty Yangtze and Jialing Rivers. With its futuristic skyline, ancient alleyways, and fiery hotpot culture, it’s a destination that demands exploration. But what if you only have three days? Fear not—this itinerary packs in the must-see landmarks, hidden gems, and unforgettable culinary experiences.

Day 1: The Urban Heart of Chongqing

Morning: Liberation Monument & Jiefangbei CBD

Start your Chongqing adventure at the Liberation Monument, the city’s symbolic center. Surrounded by neon-lit skyscrapers and luxury boutiques, this historic monument is a stark contrast to the ultra-modern Jiefangbei Central Business District. Snap photos, grab a coffee, and soak in the energy of Chongqing’s commercial hub.

Afternoon: Hongya Cave & Chaotianmen

A short walk leads you to Hongya Cave, an 11-story stilted marvel that looks straight out of a fantasy film. This labyrinth of shops, teahouses, and restaurants clings to the cliffs above the Jialing River. Don’t miss the nighttime view, but even by day, it’s a spectacle.

Next, head to Chaotianmen, where the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers converge. This is the launch point for Yangtze River cruises, but even if you’re not boarding a boat, the panoramic views are worth the trip.

Evening: Yangtze River Cable Car & Hotpot Feast

As dusk falls, ride the Yangtze River Cable Car for a bird’s-eye view of the city’s glittering skyline. Then, dive into Chongqing’s culinary soul with a spicy hotpot dinner. Try Xiaolongkan Hotpot or Dezhuang Hotpot for an authentic, tongue-numbing experience.

Day 2: History, Culture & Stunning Views

Morning: Ciqikou Ancient Town

Step back in time at Ciqikou, a 1,000-year-old riverside village. Cobblestone streets, traditional teahouses, and artisan shops selling porcelain (Ciqikou’s namesake) make it a charming escape. Sip tea, snack on mala tang (spicy skewers), and watch calligraphers at work.

Afternoon: Three Gorges Museum & Liziba Light Rail

Delve into the region’s history at the Three Gorges Museum, where exhibits cover the Yangtze’s ecology, the monumental dam project, and Chongqing’s wartime role.

Then, head to Liziba Station to witness the viral “train-through-building” phenomenon. The light rail pierces a residential high-rise, a surreal example of Chongqing’s vertical urban planning.

Evening: Nanshan Treehouse & Night Views

For sunset, hike or taxi up to Nanshan Mountain and visit the One Tree Hill Observatory or the whimsical Nanshan Treehouse. The cityscape, bathed in golden then neon light, is unforgettable. Cap the night with a cocktail at a rooftop bar like Flavor Lounge.

Day 3: Nature & Offbeat Adventures

Morning: Wulong Karst National Geology Park

Venture two hours outside the city to the Wulong Karst National Geology Park, a UNESCO site. Walk the Three Natural Bridges, towering limestone arches featured in Transformers 4, and explore the eerie Furong Cave.

Afternoon: E’ling Park & Huguang Guild Hall

Return to the city for a stroll through E’ling Park, a quiet oasis with panoramic viewpoints. Then, visit the Huguang Guild Hall, a restored Qing-dynasty complex showcasing opera stages and intricate carvings.

Evening: Jiangbei Nightlife or a River Cruise

If you’re up for more, cross to Jiangbei for chic bars like Staff Room or book a nighttime Yangtze cruise to see Hongya Cave and the skyline illuminated.

This whirlwind three-day tour captures Chongqing’s essence—its dizzying contrasts, bold flavors, and breathtaking landscapes. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or Instagrammer, Chongqing delivers. Pack comfy shoes (those hills are no joke!) and an appetite for adventure.
