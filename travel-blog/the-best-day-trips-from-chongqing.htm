The Best Day Trips from Chongqing

The magnetic, mountainous metropolis of Chongqing is a destination unto itself. A sensory overload of hotpot aromas, dazzling neon reflected in the Yangtze, and the hum of life in every stacked alleyway. But to experience the soul of this region, you must venture beyond the city’s iconic skyline. The landscapes surrounding Chongqing hold profound beauty, ancient history, and cultural wonders that contrast sharply with the urban buzz. Pack your day bag, grab your camera, and prepare to discover the incredible diversity that lies just a short journey from the city center.

Into the Depths of History & Nature

These destinations offer a journey through time, from ancient art to breathtaking natural formations carved over millennia.

The Dazu Rock Carvings: A Stone Canvas of Spiritual Harmony

Roughly a 90-minute drive west of Chongqing lies one of China’s most astonishing and underrated UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the Dazu Rock Carvings. This is not a single cave, but a series of over 50,000 intricate statues and inscriptions carved into the cliffsides and grottoes across the Dazu county, dating from the 9th to the 13th centuries.

The two most accessible and magnificent clusters are Baodingshan and Beishan. Baodingshan is the showstopper. Here, a massive, sweeping horseshoe-shaped cliff face serves as a panoramic spiritual narrative. The carvings are remarkably well-preserved and vibrantly colored, depicting scenes from Buddhist, Confucian, and Taoist beliefs living in harmonious coexistence. Look for the serene, 31-meter-long Reclining Buddha, the poignant depiction of the Cycle of Rebirth, and the vivid, almost lifelike statues of everyday people. It’s a profound and artistic achievement that rivals the more famous grottoes in Dunhuang or Luoyang, yet feels more intimate and dramatic in its hillside setting.

Wulong Karst National Geology Park: Avatar's Pandora on Earth

Made famous internationally as a filming location for the movie Avatar, the Wulong Karst is a jaw-dropping natural spectacle about a 2.5-hour drive from Chongqing. This UNESCO-listed karst landscape is a world of colossal sinkholes, natural bridges, and deep fissures.

The crown jewel is the Three Natural Bridges in the Furong Cave area. These are not man-made structures, but three immense limestone arches—Tianlong (Sky Dragon), Qinglong (Azure Dragon), and Heilong (Black Dragon)—that tower over a lush, misty gorge. You can take an elevator down into the valley and walk beneath these monumental spans, feeling utterly dwarfed by nature’s architecture. Nearby, the Furong Cave itself is a colossal underground palace filled with spectacular stalactites and stalagmites, while the Houping Tiankeng (Houping Sinkhole) offers a dizzying view into one of the world’s deepest sinkholes. The scale here is humbling and truly otherworldly.

Ancient Towns & Riverside Serenity

For a change of pace from Chongqing’s verticality, these water-adjacent towns offer labyrinthine streets, traditional architecture, and a slower rhythm of life.

Ci Qi Kou (Porcelain Village): Chongqing's Old Face

The closest and most convenient day trip, Ciqikou is practically a district of Chongqing but feels centuries apart. Perched on a hill overlooking the Jialing River, this ancient town was a major porcelain production center during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Today, its narrow, flagstone streets are lined with well-preserved wooden structures housing teahouses, souvenir shops, and snack stalls.

While it can get crowded, the charm is in the details. Find a traditional chaguan (teahouse) to sip tea and watch a Sichuan opera face-changing performance. Sample local snacks like Mao Xue Wang (a spicy hotpot of duck blood and organs) or sticky-sweet Chen Ma Po candy. Wander up to the Bao Lun Temple for a quiet view over the tiled rooftops to the river. Ciqikou is best visited early in the morning or on a weekday to capture its nostalgic atmosphere before the crowds descend.

Zhongshan Ancient Town: A Stairway to the Past

For a more authentic and less commercialized ancient town experience, head about 90 minutes south of Chongqing to Zhongshan. Built along a steep hillside on the banks of the Yangtze River tributary, this town is a masterpiece of adaptive architecture. Its main street is essentially a long, covered stairway—a multi-level "old street" sheltered by sweeping eaves, with shops and homes built into the cliff.

The vibe here is palpably historic. Elderly residents play mahjong in communal courtyards, artisans work in open-front workshops, and the scent of homemade rice wine fills the air. The town’s unique design protected it from rain and sun for centuries. A walk here feels like stepping onto a living film set, offering a genuine glimpse into riverside life before the modern era. Don’t miss the chance to try the local specialty, smoked bean curd, and to take a short boat ride on the serene river for a stunning view of the town’s cascading structure.

Thematic & Adventurous Escapes

For those seeking specific interests or a dose of adrenaline, these trips deliver unique experiences.

Chongqing's Wine Country: A Sip of the French Connection

Yes, you read that right. Nestled in the surprisingly temperate hills of Tongnan district, about 90 minutes west of central Chongqing, is the Sennong Mountain area, home to several vineyards. The most notable is the Domaine Franco-Chinois, a joint venture that has been producing surprisingly good wines for decades. The rolling vineyards, Mediterranean-style architecture, and cellar tours offer a bizarre yet delightful contrast to the Sichuan basin landscape.

A day here involves touring the vineyards, learning about the unique challenges of winemaking in this region, and of course, a tasting session. It’s a perfect, sophisticated escape for foodies and those curious about China’s burgeoning wine scene. Pair it with a meal at the estate’s restaurant for a full, leisurely day of indulgence.

Jinfo Mountain (Golden Buddha Mountain): For the Active Traveler

If you need to work off all that Chongqing hotpot, a trip to Jinfo Mountain in Nanchuan District is the answer. About two hours southeast of the city, this UNESCO-listed natural reserve is a biodiversity hotspot and a paradise for hikers. The mountain is famous for its unique square-shaped bamboo forests, ancient alpine grasslands, and spectacular karst features.

The main draw is the challenging but rewarding hike or cable car ride to the summit at 2,251 meters. On a clear day, the views over a sea of clouds and jagged peaks are breathtaking. The mountain is also home to rare animals like the clouded leopard and the adorable golden snub-nosed monkey. The temperature at the top is always refreshingly cool, making it a fantastic summer escape from Chongqing’s infamous heat. Come prepared with good hiking shoes and a jacket.

Logistics & Pro Tips for Your Chongqing Getaway

  • Transportation: For Dazu, Wulong, and Zhongshan, your best bet is the high-speed train (Gao Tie) from Chongqing’s major stations. They are fast, comfortable, and affordable. For Ciqikou, just take the metro. For the vineyards or more remote areas, consider booking a private car or joining an organized tour for maximum convenience.
  • Timing: Always leave as early as possible. Chinese domestic tourists are early risers, and beating the bulk of the crowds to sites like Dazu or Wulong significantly enhances the experience.
  • Footwear: This cannot be overstated. Whether it’s the stone steps of Ciqikou, the valley walk in Wulong, or the hike up Jinfo Mountain, comfortable, sturdy shoes are your most important packing item.
  • The Food Factor: Each destination has its local specialty. Be adventurous! From spicy tofu in Zhongshan to rustic farmhouse dishes near Jinfo Mountain, let your day trip be a culinary exploration as well.
  • Weather Readiness: Chongqing’s surrounding areas are often misty and damp. A light rain jacket or poncho is a wise addition to your day bag, along with an umbrella.

The magic of Chongqing is not confined within its municipal borders. It spills out into the dramatic river gorges, is etched into ancient stone cliffs, and whispers through the bamboo forests of its surrounding mountains. Each day trip peels back a different layer of this complex region, revealing a depth of experience that makes a visit to Chongqing truly complete. So, step out of the urban maze and let the landscapes of Chongqing municipality tell you their older, quieter, and equally compelling stories.
