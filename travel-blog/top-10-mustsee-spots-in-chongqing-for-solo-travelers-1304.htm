Top 10 Must-See Spots in Chongqing for Solo Travelers

Chongqing, China’s sprawling megacity, is a dream destination for solo travelers. With its dramatic skyline, fiery hotpot, and misty mountain vistas, this city offers an unforgettable mix of urban energy and natural beauty. Whether you're a foodie, an adventurer, or a culture seeker, Chongqing has something for everyone. Here are the top 10 must-see spots for solo explorers.

1. Hongya Cave (洪崖洞)

Why It’s a Must-See

Hongya Cave is Chongqing’s most iconic landmark—a towering, neon-lit structure built into a cliffside. By day, it resembles an ancient stilted village; by night, it transforms into a glowing fantasy straight out of a Studio Ghibli film.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Arrive before sunset to avoid crowds and snag the best photo spots.
  • Explore the maze of shops selling local crafts and snacks like lazi (spicy chili) treats.
  • The nearby Jialing River viewpoint offers stunning reflections of the illuminated buildings.

2. Liziba Monorail Station (李子坝轻轨站)

Why It’s a Must-See

Chongqing’s monorail famously pierces straight through a residential building at Liziba Station. This engineering marvel has become a viral sensation, making it a quirky must-visit.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Ride Line 2 for the full "train-through-building" experience.
  • Visit the observation deck below for the perfect Instagram shot.
  • Pair this with a stroll along the nearby Jialing River Trail for a peaceful escape.

3. Ciqikou Ancient Town (磁器口古镇)

Why It’s a Must-See

Step back in time at Ciqikou, a 1,000-year-old riverside village packed with teahouses, street food stalls, and traditional Sichuan opera performances.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Try mala tang (spicy skewers) and jiangtuan (spicy fish) from street vendors.
  • Visit early morning to avoid the midday tourist rush.
  • Hidden alleyways lead to quieter artisan workshops—perfect for unique souvenirs.

4. E’ling Park (鹅岭公园)

Why It’s a Must-See

This serene hilltop park offers panoramic views of Chongqing’s skyline and the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Climb the Liangjiang Pavilion for the best vantage point.
  • The park is especially magical at dusk when the city lights begin to flicker.
  • Nearby E’ling Creative Park has hip cafés ideal for solo downtime.

5. Yangtze River Cableway (长江索道)

Why It’s a Must-See

Glide over the Yangtze River in a vintage cable car—once a daily commute for locals, now a thrilling ride with unbeatable city views.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Buy tickets online to skip long queues.
  • Sunset rides offer golden-hour photo ops.
  • Combine with a visit to Hongya Cave for a full riverside experience.

6. Wulong Karst National Geology Park (武隆喀斯特)

Why It’s a Must-See

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Wulong’s otherworldly karst landscapes include the Three Natural Bridges and Furong Cave.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Join a day tour from Chongqing (about 3 hours by bus).
  • Wear sturdy shoes—the terrain is rugged but rewarding.
  • The park’s misty valleys feel like something from Avatar.

7. Jiefangbei Pedestrian Street (解放碑步行街)

Why It’s a Must-See

Chongqing’s Times Square, Jiefangbei is the heart of the city’s shopping, dining, and nightlife.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Visit the Liberation Monument at night when it’s lit up.
  • Foodies: Don’t miss Hao Chi Jie (Good Eat Street) for local bites.
  • Nearby Luohanzuan Temple offers a quiet contrast to the bustle.

8. Dazu Rock Carvings (大足石刻)

Why It’s a Must-See

These 9th-century Buddhist cliff carvings are a masterpiece of religious art, less crowded than similar sites like Longmen or Mogao.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Take a bus from Chongqing (2-hour ride).
  • Hire an audio guide to appreciate the intricate storytelling.
  • The Baoding Mountain section is the most impressive.

9. Nanshan Botanical Garden (南山植物园)

Why It’s a Must-See

Escape the urban jungle in this lush garden, home to exotic plants and sweeping views of Chongqing.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Spring visits reward you with cherry blossoms.
  • Hike up Nanshan Mountain for a quiet retreat.
  • The One Tree Pavilion is a prime sunset spot.

10. Chaotianmen Square (朝天门广场)

Why It’s a Must-See

Where the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers meet, Chaotianmen is the launch point for scenic cruises and a symbol of Chongqing’s maritime history.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Book an evening river cruise for dazzling skyline views.
  • The newly opened Lai Fook Centre nearby has a rooftop observatory.
  • Street vendors here sell xiaomian (Chongqing noodles)—try a bowl!

Chongqing is a solo traveler’s paradise, blending futuristic cityscapes with ancient charm. From dizzying monorails to fiery street food, every corner promises adventure. Pack light, embrace the mala spice, and let this mountain city surprise you.
