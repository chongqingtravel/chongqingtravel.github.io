Chongqing’s Best Hiking Trails with Overnight Camping Spots

Chongqing, a sprawling metropolis in southwestern China, is famous for its spicy hotpot, dramatic skyline, and the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers. But beyond the urban buzz lies a hiker’s paradise—rugged mountains, lush forests, and hidden trails perfect for overnight camping adventures. Whether you're a seasoned trekker or a weekend warrior, these trails offer breathtaking views, serene nature, and unforgettable experiences.

The Top 5 Hiking Trails in Chongqing

1. Jinfo Mountain (金佛山) – The King of Karst Landscapes

Jinfo Mountain, a UNESCO World Heritage Site candidate, is a must-visit for hikers seeking dramatic karst formations and alpine meadows. The trails here range from moderate to challenging, with plenty of scenic overlooks.

Best Overnight Camping Spot: Near the Golden Buddha Peak, where you can pitch a tent and wake up to a sea of clouds at sunrise. The area has flat, grassy patches ideal for camping, though temperatures drop significantly at night—pack warm!

Pro Tip: Visit in late spring or early autumn to avoid extreme weather. The mountain is also home to rare flora and fauna, so keep an eye out for wild orchids and golden monkeys.

2. Simian Mountain (四面山) – Waterfalls and Wild Terrain

Simian Mountain, meaning "Four-Faced Mountain," is a hidden gem with cascading waterfalls, dense forests, and deep valleys. The Wangxiangtai Waterfall, one of China’s tallest, is a highlight.

Best Overnight Camping Spot: Honghai Lake offers lakeside camping with stunning reflections of the surrounding peaks. The area is peaceful, with clean water sources nearby.

Trail Difficulty: Moderate, with some steep sections. The Lover’s Ladder is a thrilling (and slightly vertigo-inducing) climb with metal rungs bolted into the cliffside.

3. Wulong Karst National Geology Park (武隆喀斯特) – Otherworldly Scenery

Famous for its dramatic sinkholes and limestone caves, Wulong is a surreal hiking destination. The Three Natural Bridges (Tianlong, Qinglong, and Heilong) are a must-see.

Best Overnight Camping Spot: Near Furong Cave, where you can camp under a star-filled sky. The cave itself is illuminated at night, creating an eerie yet magical ambiance.

Bonus: If you’re a film buff, you might recognize Wulong from Transformers: Age of Extinction—the towering karst formations were a filming location.

4. Nanshan Mountain (南山) – A Quick Escape from the City

For those short on time, Nanshan Mountain offers a convenient getaway with panoramic views of Chongqing’s skyline. The trails are well-marked and suitable for beginners.

Best Overnight Camping Spot: One Tree Hill, a quiet spot with minimal light pollution—perfect for stargazing. You’ll also find small vendors selling local snacks like lazi ji (spicy chicken) if you need a quick bite.

Night Hike Option: The mountain is popular for night hikes, with the city lights twinkling below like a sea of stars.

5. Tie Shan Ping (铁山坪) – Forest Bathing and Hot Springs

Tie Shan Ping is a forested retreat with well-maintained trails and a tranquil atmosphere. The Jiangjun Ridge Trail is a favorite, offering sweeping views of the Yangtze River.

Best Overnight Camping Spot: Near the Tie Shan Ping Hot Springs, where you can soak your muscles after a long hike. Some campsites even offer private hot spring access—luxury camping at its finest!

Local Flavor: Don’t miss the chance to try jiangtuan yu (a local river fish dish) at nearby farmhouses.

Essential Camping Tips for Chongqing’s Trails

Gear Checklist

  • Tent & Sleeping Bag: Nights can get chilly, even in summer.
  • Waterproof Layers: Chongqing is notoriously humid, with sudden rain showers.
  • Portable Stove: While some trails have food vendors, it’s best to be self-sufficient.
  • Bug Spray: Mosquitoes and ticks are common in wooded areas.

Permits and Regulations

Some trails, like Jinfo Mountain, require entry permits. Check local regulations before heading out, especially if you plan to camp in protected areas.

Leave No Trace

Chongqing’s natural beauty is fragile. Pack out all trash, avoid disturbing wildlife, and use established campsites whenever possible.

Off-the-Beaten-Path: Lesser-Known Trails

Fengjie Tiankeng (奉节天坑) – The Giant Sinkhole

This remote trail leads to one of the world’s deepest sinkholes. The hike is strenuous but rewards you with jaw-dropping views.

Camping Tip: Set up near Xiaozhai Tiankeng for an unforgettable night under the stars.

Wushan’s Little Three Gorges (巫山小三峡) – Riverside Hiking

A boat ride followed by a hike along emerald-green waters makes this trail unique. Camp on small sandbars by the river for a truly immersive experience.

Whether you’re chasing waterfalls, summiting peaks, or simply escaping the city, Chongqing’s hiking trails offer something for every adventurer. Just remember to respect nature, pack wisely, and embrace the unexpected—because the best stories start where the pavement ends.
