Chongqing Itinerary: Exploring Ciqikou Ancient Town

Nestled along the banks of the Jialing River, Ciqikou Ancient Town is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Chongqing. With its labyrinthine alleyways, traditional architecture, and vibrant local culture, this 1,000-year-old town offers a fascinating glimpse into China’s past while remaining a lively hub for food, shopping, and history. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or just looking for a picturesque escape from the city’s skyscrapers, Ciqikou delivers an unforgettable experience.

Why Ciqikou Should Be on Your Chongqing Itinerary

A Living Piece of Chongqing’s History

Ciqikou, which translates to "Porcelain Village," was once a bustling port during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Its name comes from the porcelain workshops that thrived here, supplying fine ceramics to the region. Today, the town retains its old-world charm with well-preserved wooden buildings, stone-paved streets, and hidden courtyards. Walking through Ciqikou feels like stepping back in time—especially when you stumble upon a traditional tea house or a family-run handicraft shop.

A Food Lover’s Paradise

No visit to Ciqikou is complete without indulging in its legendary street food. From spicy Chongqing hotpot to sweet mala tang (spicy skewers), the aromas wafting through the alleys are irresistible. Don’t miss these must-try snacks:

  • Chen Mahua (Twisted Dough Crunch): A crispy, caramelized pastry that’s been a local favorite for centuries.
  • Suan La Fen (Hot and Sour Rice Noodles): A fiery, tangy bowl of noodles that packs a punch.
  • Jiang Zhuang Baba (Sticky Rice Cake): Glutinous rice cakes stuffed with sweet or savory fillings.

Pro tip: Visit in the evening when the food stalls come alive with sizzling woks and steaming baskets.

Top Things to Do in Ciqikou

Wander the Ancient Streets

The heart of Ciqikou is its maze-like streets, each lined with red lanterns and wooden storefronts. Key spots to explore include:

  • Main Street (Zheng Jie): The busiest thoroughfare, packed with souvenir shops and snack vendors.
  • Huangjueping Lane: A quieter alley filled with artisan studios and quaint cafés.
  • Baolun Temple: A serene Buddhist temple offering panoramic views of the river.

Experience Traditional Tea Culture

Tea houses are a cornerstone of Ciqikou’s culture. Head to Shu Fang Zhai Tea House, where you can sip Pu’er tea while watching a live Sichuan opera performance. The rhythmic clinking of teacups and the hum of local chatter create an ambiance that’s hard to replicate elsewhere.

Shop for Unique Souvenirs

Skip the mass-produced trinkets and hunt for authentic keepsakes:

  • Hand-Painted Porcelain: A nod to the town’s porcelain-making heritage.
  • Embroidery Art: Intricate designs by local artisans.
  • Spice Blends: Take home the flavors of Chongqing with pre-packaged huajiao (Sichuan peppercorns).

Hidden Gems & Off-the-Beaten-Path Spots

The Lesser-Known Riverside Walk

Most tourists stick to the main streets, but the path along the Jialing River offers stunning views and a peaceful retreat. It’s especially magical at sunset when the water reflects the golden hues of the sky.

The Secret Courtyard Cafés

Tucked away behind unassuming doors, cafés like Ming Yao Xuan offer a quiet escape with strong coffee and homemade desserts. Their rooftop seating is perfect for snapping photos of the town’s rooftops.

Practical Tips for Visiting Ciqikou

Best Time to Visit

  • Morning (8-10 AM): Beat the crowds and enjoy the misty, atmospheric streets.
  • Weekdays: Weekends can get overwhelmingly busy.

Getting There

Ciqikou is easily accessible via Chongqing’s Metro Line 1 (Ciqikou Station). Taxis are also affordable, but traffic can be heavy.

What to Wear

Comfortable shoes are a must—the cobblestone streets are uneven. In summer, bring a hat and sunscreen; in winter, layer up as the river breeze can be chilly.

Final Thoughts

Ciqikou is more than just a tourist stop—it’s a living, breathing slice of Chongqing’s soul. Whether you’re savoring a bowl of xiaomian (spicy noodles), bargaining for handmade crafts, or simply soaking in the historic vibe, this ancient town promises an adventure that lingers long after you leave.
