Chongqing & Nearby Day Trips: 6-Day Itinerary

Chongqing, China’s sprawling megacity, is a mesmerizing blend of modernity and tradition, nestled among misty mountains and divided by the mighty Yangtze and Jialing Rivers. Known for its spicy hotpot, dramatic skyline, and rich cultural heritage, Chongqing also serves as a gateway to some of China’s most breathtaking natural wonders. This 6-day itinerary combines urban exploration with unforgettable day trips to nearby highlights, ensuring a well-rounded adventure.

Day 1: Exploring Chongqing’s Urban Charms

Morning: Hongya Cave & Jiefangbei

Start your journey at Hongya Cave, an iconic stilted building complex that lights up like a fairy tale at night. By day, explore its labyrinth of shops, teahouses, and local snack stalls. Don’t miss the chance to try xiaomian (Chongqing noodles) or mala tang (spicy skewers).

Next, head to Jiefangbei, Chongqing’s bustling commercial heart. This pedestrian square is surrounded by glittering malls and historic landmarks, including the People’s Liberation Monument.

Afternoon: Ciqikou Ancient Town

A short metro ride away, Ciqikou Ancient Town offers a glimpse into old Chongqing. Wander its narrow alleys lined with Ming- and Qing-era architecture, and sample local treats like Chen Mahua (fried dough twists) or sip tea in a traditional chaguan (teahouse).

Evening: Yangtze River Cable Car & Night Views

Cap off the day with a ride on the Yangtze River Cable Car, soaring above the water for panoramic city views. For dinner, indulge in Chongqing’s famed hotpot—head to Xiaolongkan or Dezhuang for an authentic experience.

Day 2: Dazu Rock Carvings Day Trip

Morning: Journey to Dazu

Just two hours from Chongqing, the Dazu Rock Carvings are a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring thousands of intricate Buddhist, Taoist, and Confucian sculptures carved into cliffs. The Baodingshan and Beishan grottoes are the highlights, with vivid depictions of religious lore.

Afternoon: Local Flavors & Return

After exploring, grab lunch at a nearby village restaurant—try Dazu kao ya (roast duck) or suancai yu (pickled fish). Return to Chongqing by late afternoon and unwind at Nanshan Botanical Garden or a riverside bar.

Day 3: Wulong Karst National Park Adventure

Full-Day Trip: Wulong’s Natural Wonders

A 3-hour drive brings you to Wulong Karst National Park, a filming location for Transformers 4. Highlights include:
- Three Natural Bridges: Towering limestone arches shrouded in mist.
- Furong Cave: An underground marvel with colossal stalactites.
- Longshuixia Fissure Gorge: A dramatic canyon perfect for hiking.

Pack sturdy shoes and a raincoat—the area is often damp and slippery.

Day 4: Fengjie & the Three Gorges

Morning: Qutang Gorge & White Emperor City

Travel to Fengjie County (4 hours by car) to explore Qutang Gorge, the narrowest of the Three Gorges. Visit White Emperor City, perched on a hilltop with sweeping river views and ties to ancient poetry.

Afternoon: Yangtze River Cruise (Short Option)

Opt for a short Yangtze River cruise to witness the gorge’s sheer cliffs. Alternatively, hike the Kuimen Trail for postcard-perfect vistas. Return to Chongqing by evening.

Day 5: Leshan Giant Buddha Excursion

Morning: High-Speed Train to Leshan

Take a 2-hour high-speed train to Leshan, home to the world’s largest stone Buddha. The Leshan Giant Buddha, carved into a riverside cliff, stands 71 meters tall. Climb the stairway for close-up views or take a boat for the full perspective.

Afternoon: Lingyun Temple & Local Eats

Explore Lingyun Temple nearby, then savor Leshan specialties like tiao tiao ji (boneless chicken) or bobo ji (spicy chicken salad). Return to Chongqing by train.

Day 6: Relaxing in Chongqing’s Hinterland

Morning: Jindao Lake Scenic Area

Escape the city to Jindao Lake, a serene retreat surrounded by forested hills. Rent a bike, paddle a boat, or simply picnic by the water.

Afternoon: Hot Springs & Farewell Feast

Unwind at Beibei Hot Springs before a final feast. Try la zi ji (chili-fried chicken) or shui zhu yu (poached fish in chili broth).

This itinerary balances Chongqing’s urban energy with the natural and cultural gems just beyond its borders. Whether you’re a foodie, history buff, or nature lover, this 6-day adventure promises memories to last a lifetime.
